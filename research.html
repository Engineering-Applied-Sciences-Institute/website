Our research

We focus on modeling, estimation, and control of complex uncertain systems, with active projects in space systems and an emerging effort in computational biology. We plan to expand into other domains over time.

Research projects

Satellite-to-Satellite Power Beaming

We are developing and researching wireless power beaming between spacecraft in orbit. This work aims to enable new capabilities for space infrastructure by transferring power from one satellite to another without physical connection.

Remote Dynamics Characterization for Uncooperative Bodies in Space

We work on characterizing the dynamics of tumbling objects—relevant for space debris, rendezvous, and other scenarios involving complex rigid-body motion. This supports our broader focus on modeling, estimation, and control of complex uncertain systems.

Computational Biology for Medical Applications

An emerging project applying networking and dynamical systems approaches to biological systems, with applications in medicine. We are exploring how these methods can yield insight into biological networks and dynamics; the precise direction of the work is still taking shape.
